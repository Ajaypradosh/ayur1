IDUKKI

The cradle of spices that have journeyed the world, enriching flavors and lives alike.

With its towering hills and stunning waterfalls, Idukki is a treasure trove of spices, many of which
are native to the region. Nestled in the Western Ghats, this district in Kerala, southern India, derives
its name from the Malayalam word “Idukk,” meaning gorge. True to its name, Idukki's landscape is marked by mountains encircling fertile plains and rivers weaving through them. It also shares a border with the neighboring state of Tamil Nadu.

The district's elevation varies from 130m to 1300m above sea level, contributing to its diverse geography and climate.
This variety supports a rich assortment of spices and crops. Idukki is broadly divided into high ranges (above 600m) and low ranges (below 600m). The famed Cardamom Hills, rising above 900m, are the heart of green cardamom cultivation.
